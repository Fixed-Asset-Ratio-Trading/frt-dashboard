What are "sats"?

A "sat" (short for satoshi) is the smallest unit of Bitcoin, named after Bitcoin's pseudonymous creator, Satoshi Nakamoto.

1 Bitcoin = 100,000,000 sats
1 sat = 0.00000001 BTC

Instead of thinking in confusing decimal notation like 0.00001 BTC, you can think in whole numbers like 1,000 sats. This makes tracking your Bitcoin growth more intuitive and motivating!

What backs Satoshi tokens?
Every tSAT token is backed 1:1 by Threshold Network's tBTC, which itself is backed 1:1 by Bitcoin. The backing is held in an immutable smart contract with verifiable on-chain reserves that anyone can audit in real-time.
How fast can I redeem tSAT for tBTC?
Redemption is instant through the Fixed Ratio Trading protocol. Simply swap your tSAT tokens for tBTC at the guaranteed 1:100,000,000 ratio whenever liquidity is available in the pool.
Are returns promised?
No—tSAT is a fully backed utility token, not a yield product. It maintains a 1:1 peg with Bitcoin satoshis and does not promise any returns beyond Bitcoin's natural price movements.
There are only 1% of all Bitcoin can become tSAT - what happens after that?
The maximum supply is capped at 21 trillion tSAT (equivalent to 210,000 BTC or 1% of Bitcoin's total supply). Once this limit is reached, no new tSAT can be minted. Market forces will balance supply and demand through price discovery.
How do I verify the reserves?
All reserves are verifiable on-chain. You can check the Fixed Ratio Trading pool on Solscan at any time to see exactly how much tBTC backs the circulating tSAT supply. The smart contract code is open source and auditable by anyone.
What are the fees?
Trading tSAT through the Fixed Ratio Trading protocol currently has 0% pool fees (governance-controlled). You only pay standard Solana network fees for transactions, which are typically fractions of a cent.
Why is liquidity not locked?

Locking liquidity is actually counterproductive for this type of protocol. Here's why tSAT liquidity remains accessible:

  • Capital efficiency: This represents real working capital. If locked, the value becomes inaccessible forever, reducing the protocol's ability to maintain the 1:1 peg and serve users.
  • Fee management: In locked liquidity scenarios, trading fees accumulate but cannot be redistributed or managed. Over time, these fees would compound exponentially, eventually consuming the entire tSAT supply through inefficiency.
  • Redeemability guarantee: Unlike speculative tokens, tSAT must maintain instant redeemability for tBTC. Locked liquidity would prevent users from redeeming their tokens, breaking the core promise of 1:1 backing.
  • Protocol flexibility: Active liquidity management allows the protocol to respond to market conditions, upgrade to better liquidity solutions, and maintain optimal trading conditions for users.
  • Transparent reserves: All reserves are verifiable on-chain in real-time. The LP tokens are burned, permanently committing the reserves to the protocol, but the underlying assets remain functional rather than frozen.

The Fixed Ratio Trading protocol uses burned LP tokens to ensure reserves cannot be withdrawn while maintaining the flexibility needed for a fully-backed, redeemable token system.

Why should anyone buy tSAT instead of bitcoin?

tSAT isn't meant to replace Bitcoin—it's designed to make Bitcoin more accessible and useful. Here's why you might choose tSAT:

  • Psychological clarity: Track your holdings in whole numbers (100,000 sats) instead of confusing decimals (0.001 BTC). This makes Bitcoin accumulation feel more tangible and motivating.
  • Solana speed and cost: Transfer your Bitcoin-backed value in seconds with fees under $0.01, compared to Bitcoin network fees that can exceed $5-50 during congestion. Perfect for everyday transactions.
  • DeFi integration: Use your Bitcoin in Solana's DeFi ecosystem—lending, borrowing, trading—while maintaining full Bitcoin exposure through the 1:1 tBTC backing.
  • Instant redeemability: Unlike Bitcoin bridges that require hours of confirmations, tSAT converts to tBTC (and ultimately BTC) in seconds through the Fixed Ratio Trading protocol with guaranteed 1:1 exchange.
  • No custody risk: Your tSAT is backed by Threshold Network's tBTC, which is secured by a decentralized network of signers—no single custodian can run away with your funds.
  • Same Bitcoin exposure: You get 100% Bitcoin price exposure. When Bitcoin goes up 10%, your tSAT value (in BTC terms) stays perfectly aligned through the fixed 1:100,000,000 ratio.

Best use case: Buy Bitcoin, hold the majority in cold storage for long-term savings, but keep some as tSAT for active use—payments, DeFi, or quick transfers—knowing you can always convert back to BTC instantly at the guaranteed ratio.
