📰 Media Guidelines for Satoshi Token

For Media, Publications, and Content Creators

This guide explains how to properly refer to and represent the Satoshi Token (tSAT) in your articles, publications, and social media content.

✍️ Proper Ticker Symbol Usage

Official Ticker Symbol: tSAT

  • t = lowercase
  • SAT = all uppercase

Recommended Usage in Different Contexts:

Context Correct Format Example
Twitter / Social Media $tSAT "Just bought 1M $tSAT tokens!"
Publications / Articles $tSAT or tSAT "The tSAT token represents satoshis on Solana"
Technical Documentation tSAT "Each tSAT equals 1 satoshi"
Full Name Satoshi Token (tSAT) "Satoshi Token (tSAT) launched today"

❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid:

TSAT ← Wrong (all uppercase)
tsat ← Wrong (all lowercase)
Tsat ← Wrong (capital T)
TSat ← Wrong (capital T and S)
tSAT ← Correct! ✓

🖼️ Image Assets & Branding

There are two distinct image sets for different use cases. Understanding when to use each is important for accurate representation.

1. Satoshi Images (Orange "S" Logo)

Usage: Use these images when referring to "satoshis" in general — the smallest unit of Bitcoin (1/100,000,000 of a bitcoin).

Visual Identity:

  • Orange "S" angled at 13.88 degrees
  • One dot above and one dot below the "S"
  • No plus sign or additional symbols
  • Represents the universal concept of satoshis

2. tSatoshi Images (With Plus Sign)

Usage: Use these images when specifically referring to the tSAT token on Solana (or any tokenized representation).

Visual Identity:

  • Orange "S" angled at 13.88 degrees (same as above)
  • Black squares arranged in a plus sign (+) pattern to the left of the "S"
  • White square in the center of the plus sign
  • Represents tokenized satoshis (similar to tBTC branding)

Why the Plus Sign?

The "+" symbol indicates this is a tokenized version, similar to how tBTC uses the "t" prefix. This is why our ticker is $tSAT (not $SAT) — the "t" stands for "token" or "threshold," matching the tBTC convention.

🔮 Future Plans: The Satoshi Chain

Important Note for Long-Term Coverage:

We maintain separate branding (Satoshi vs. tSatoshi) because we have plans to create a Satoshi Chain — a Bitcoin second-layer solution that will be different from what we have today.

What's Coming:

  • Satoshi Chain: A new blockchain specifically designed for satoshi transactions
  • $SAT Token: The native token of Satoshi Chain (using the Satoshi images without the plus sign)
  • Wormhole Integration: Satoshi Chain will bridge to Solana and other blockchains
  • Bitcoin Second Layer: Direct integration with Bitcoin as a second-layer solution
  • Development Funding: All funds collected from tSAT support development of Satoshi Chain and related projects

Our Mission:

"To provide a secure, cheap, and fast method for everyone to use Bitcoin — one way or another."
